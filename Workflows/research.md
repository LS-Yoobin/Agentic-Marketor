# Research Workflow

This workflow is triggered when the user asks you to research any topic. Follow each phase in order. Do not skip phases or rush to research before completing intake.

---

## PHASE 1: INTAKE

Before researching, assess the topic complexity:

- **Simple topic** — narrow, well-defined, single-domain (e.g. "what is compound interest") → ask 3 core questions
- **Complex topic** — broad, multi-faceted, ambiguous, or cross-domain (e.g. "the future of creator monetization") → ask up to 6 questions

**Core questions — always ask these, one at a time, waiting for the answer before asking the next:**

1. **Scope** — What specifically do you want to know about this topic? Are there any angles or subtopics you want to exclude?
2. **Audience** — Who is this report for? (just you, your co-founder/team, or will it be published publicly?)
3. **Intent** — What will you do with this research? (inform a product decision, write a blog post, general curiosity, something else?)

**Extended questions — ask only for complex topics, continuing one at a time:**

4. **Prior knowledge** — What do you already know about this topic? (helps avoid retreading familiar ground)
5. **Recency** — Do you need the most cutting-edge information, or is foundational and historical context equally valuable?
6. **Format preferences** — Are there any sections you specifically want included or excluded from the final report?

Do not ask all questions at once. Ask one, wait for the answer, then ask the next.

---

## PHASE 2: RESEARCH

Conduct thorough research targeting **20 or more distinct sources**. Cover all four layers:

- **Foundational** — definitions, background context, core concepts, history of the topic
- **Current landscape** — recent developments, trends, market data, news (prioritize last 1-3 years)
- **Multiple angles** — expert opinions, practitioner perspectives, criticism and counterpoints, real-world case examples
- **Data & statistics** — specific numbers, studies, surveys, and quantitative findings to anchor claims

Before moving to synthesis, self-check:
- [ ] 20+ distinct sources have been consulted
- [ ] No single source dominates more than ~20% of the content
- [ ] Both mainstream and niche/expert sources are represented
- [ ] Contradictions or disagreements in the literature have been noted, not ignored or flattened

If the self-check fails, continue researching before proceeding.

---

## PHASE 3: SYNTHESIS

Before writing the report, internally organize your findings:

- Group related findings by theme (not by source or chronologically)
- Identify the 3-5 most important insights across all sources
- Flag any contradictions, gaps, or unresolved debates in the research
- Determine the best section order given the audience and intent stated in intake

This phase is internal. The user does not need to see the outline — only the final report.

---

## PHASE 4: OUTPUT

Write the final report using the structure below. Adapt section depth to the topic — short and tight for focused topics, more expansive for complex ones.

---

```
# [Topic] — Research Report
*[Date] | Audience: [who it's for] | Depth: Deep Dive (20+ sources)*

## Executive Summary
2-3 sentences. The single most important finding or insight from the research.

## Background
Foundational context — what this topic is, why it matters, relevant history.

## Current Landscape
What's happening right now — trends, market movements, recent developments and data.

## Key Findings
3-6 thematic sections, each covering a distinct angle or insight.
Each section: state the key insight, provide supporting evidence, cite sources inline.

## Contradictions & Open Questions
Where experts disagree, where data is thin, or where the field is still evolving.

## Key Takeaways
5-7 bullet points — the most important things to remember from this research.

## Recommended Next Steps
3-5 actionable items tailored to the user's stated intent and audience.
Be specific — "read X", "test assumption Y", "reach out to Z type of person", etc.

## Sources
Numbered list of all sources cited in the report.
```

---

**Save the report to:** `output/research-[topic-slug]-YYYY-MM-DD.md`

Example: `output/research-creator-economy-2026-04-11.md`

Use today's date. Use lowercase kebab-case for the topic slug, keeping it short (3-5 words max).
