# Skill: Marketing Research & Planning for Bloggo

## When to use this skill
Use this whenever you are asked to:
- Research competitors or the market landscape
- Write or improve marketing copy
- Plan a campaign, launch, or marketing strategy
- Develop App Store descriptions or metadata
- Create user personas or audience segments
- Write social media content
- Analyze trends in travel, photography, or AI apps

---

## Step 1: Ground yourself in Bloggo's context

Before doing any research or writing, read these two documents:

1. `docs/marketing/bloggo-product-context.md` — What the app does, features, user journey, monetization
2. `docs/marketing/bloggo-brand.md` — Voice, tone, messaging rules, positioning, what we are and are not

Do not write a single word of copy or make a single recommendation until you have read both. These are your constraints.

---

## Step 2: Clarify the task

Ask yourself (or the user):
- What is the **goal**? (awareness, downloads, conversion, retention, press?)
- Who is the **audience**? (new users, existing users, press, investors, App Store visitors?)
- What is the **format**? (social post, App Store description, blog post, strategy doc, ad copy?)
- What is the **time horizon**? (for launch, ongoing, seasonal?)

If any of these are unclear, ask before proceeding.

---

## Step 3: Research framework (for market/competitor research)

When researching the competitive landscape or market, always cover:

### A. Direct Competitors
Apps that do something similar to Bloggo. Look for:
- Name, category, App Store rating, price
- Core value proposition
- What they do better than Bloggo
- What Bloggo does better than them
- Their marketing angle and tone

**Known competitors to investigate:**
- Polarsteps (travel tracking + journal)
- Day One (journal with photos)
- Google Photos (memories/albums)
- Apple Journal
- Unfold (story-style social content)
- Canva (for travel content creation)
- Any new AI travel/journal apps

### B. Adjacent Markets
What other categories are Bloggo users drawn to?
- Travel planning apps (TripIt, TripAdvisor)
- Social travel (Instagram, BeReal)
- Memory/nostalgia apps
- AI writing assistants

### C. Market Trends
What's happening in the broader landscape?
- AI-generated content adoption
- Travel recovery and growth trends
- Privacy-first feature demand
- Short-form vs. long-form content behavior
- On-device AI as a selling point

### D. Audience Insights
Where does our target audience spend time?
- Social platforms they use
- Travel content creators they follow
- Keywords they search
- Pain points expressed in reviews of competitor apps

## Step 4: Writing & copy framework

When writing any copy for Bloggo:

1. **Start with the user's emotion**, not the feature
   - What are they feeling before Bloggo? (overwhelmed, guilty, nostalgic)
   - What do they feel after? (satisfied, proud, connected)

2. **Lead with the outcome**
   - "Turn your travel photos into a beautiful blog in seconds" ✓
   - "Bloggo uses AI to scan your photo library" ✗

3. **Apply the brand voice rules** (from bloggo-brand.md)
   - Warm, confident, simple, delightful
   - No jargon, no passive voice, no cloud mentions (V1)

4. **Check against existing copy**
   - Does it sound like Bloggo? Compare with the "Existing Marketing Copy" table in the product context doc
   - If it sounds like a different brand, rewrite it

5. **Always provide 2–3 variations**
   - Give options from punchy/short to more descriptive
   - Label them (e.g., "Short form," "App Store," "Social")

## Step 5: Strategy & planning framework

When developing a marketing plan or launch strategy:

### Structure every plan with:
1. **Objective** — One clear measurable goal (e.g., "1,000 downloads in first 30 days")
2. **Audience** — Which persona(s) are we targeting?
3. **Channels** — Where will we reach them? (App Store, social, PR, influencers, word of mouth)
4. **Message** — What is the single core message for this campaign?
5. **Content plan** — What specific assets or posts are needed?
6. **Timeline** — When does each piece go out?
7. **Success metrics** — How will we know if it worked?

### For App Store launch specifically:
- Title (30 char max)
- Subtitle (30 char max)
- Description (4,000 char max — first 3 lines are most important)
- Keywords (100 char max, comma-separated)
- Screenshots (6.5" and 5.5" required; show benefit in headline, not just UI)
- Preview video (optional but high-impact)

---

## Step 6: Output format

Always deliver research and plans in clean, structured markdown with:
- Clear section headers
- Bullet points for lists
- Tables for comparisons
- Bold for key takeaways
- A "Recommended next steps" section at the end

---

## Constraints to always respect

- No cloud feature mentions in V1 marketing
- On-device AI / privacy is a key selling point — always mention it
- The app is iOS only — do not imply Android availability
- Pricing is TBD — do not invent numbers
- "Bloggo" is always capitalized
- The Vibe feature (ambient audio) is a unique differentiator — use it
