# Bloggo — Talking Head Video Scripts
**Notion destination:** `notion` → Bloggo Content Engine
**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Purpose:** App launch content — Bloggo TikTok / Instagram Reels

---

## FILMING NOTES (applies to all videos)

**Setup**
- Natural window light — soft, not harsh. Sit facing the window.
- Clean background: plain wall, bookshelf, or slightly blurred room. Nothing distracting.
- Phone or gimbal, slightly below eye level, angled up. Feels natural, not corporate.
- Slight lean-in for intimacy on emotional beats.

**Tone**
- Founder energy, not ad energy. Like you're telling a friend about something real.
- Pause between sentences. Let the ideas breathe.
- No reading off a screen — internalize the script, then film naturally.
- Imperfect is good. Authenticity beats polish.

**On-Screen Text**
- Add bold text overlays on key words using CapCut, Instagram, or TikTok editor after filming.
- Specific suggestions included per script.

# ⚡ 10-SECOND SCRIPTS

---

## 10s #1 — "Stop Letting Them Collect Dust"
**Hook type:** Confrontation / Pattern interrupt

**Scene:** Close-up face, natural light. Direct eye contact. Slight knowing smile. No props needed.

**Script:**
> "How many photos from your last trip have you actually done anything with?
> [pause — let them answer in their head]
> That's exactly what Bloggo is for. Link in bio."

**Delivery:** Calm. Like you already know the answer and so do they. The pause is the punchline.

**On-screen text:**
- `your camera roll: 5,000 photos` → `your blog: 0`
- End card: `Bloggo — link in bio`

---

## 10s #2 — "Every Photo Had a Reason"
**Hook type:** Emotional / philosophical

**Scene:** Hold up your phone facing camera, like you're scrolling old trip photos. Look up. Speak directly.

**Script:**
> "Every photo you've ever taken had a reason.
> A moment. A feeling. Something worth keeping.
> [beat]
> Bloggo makes sure you never forget it."

**Delivery:** Slow. Meaningful. Don't rush. The pauses are doing the work.

**On-screen text:**
- `every photo had a reason`
- `Bloggo remembers it.`

---

## 10s #3 — "You Just Got Home"
**Hook type:** Relatable scenario

**Scene:** Sitting casually. Travel bag or luggage visible in background — suggests just returned. Slightly tired but happy energy.

**Script:**
> "You just got home.
> [hold up fingers, counting] 1,200 photos. Zero words written.
> [beat, dry smile]
> Bloggo turns that into a full travel blog. In 60 seconds."

**Delivery:** Deadpan. The contrast between the numbers is the joke. Don't over-sell the last line.

**On-screen text:**
- `1,200 photos.`
- `0 words written.`
- `Bloggo.`

---

## 10s #4 — "The Blank Page"
**Hook type:** Relatable frustration / comedy

**Scene:** Direct to camera. Maybe at a desk with laptop visible (but screen not shown). Knowing smile.

**Script:**
> "You want to write about your trip.
> You open a blank page.
> [pause, smiles]
> You close it.
> [beat]
> Bloggo removes the blank page. Entirely."

**Delivery:** Self-aware, warm humor. Like you've personally done this a hundred times.

**On-screen text:**
- `*opens blank document*`
- `*immediately closes it*`
- `Bloggo removes the blank page.`

---

## 10s #5 — "Blog While You Travel"
**Hook type:** Feature reveal / "what if" hook

**Scene:** Hold phone naturally. Can be outdoors or at a table. Energetic, forward-leaning.

**Script:**
> "What if your travel blog was already written
> by the time you got home?
> [show phone briefly]
> Blog as you go. Bloggo captures everything live.
> Link in bio."

**Delivery:** Excited but grounded. The idea should feel surprising but obvious.

**On-screen text:**
- `what if your blog wrote itself?`
- `Blog on the Go — Bloggo`

---

## 10s #6 — "Your Blog Becomes Your Content"
**Hook type:** Feature reveal — slides export

**Scene:** Phone in hand, mimicking scrolling through slides. Then look up to camera.

**Script:**
> "After Bloggo builds your travel blog,
> it generates ready-to-post slides —
> your place names, your captions, already there.
> 4:5 for the feed. 9:16 for Stories.
> Your blog makes your content."

**Delivery:** Clean and punchy. Feature-reveal energy. Let each line land.

**On-screen text:**
- `your blog →` `your Instagram content`
- `4:5 + 9:16 formats`
- `place names + captions auto-included`

# 🎬 30-SECOND SCRIPTS

---

## 30s #1 — "Let Me Introduce Bloggo" *(Main Launch Video)*
**Hook type:** Direct founder intro
**Use this as:** The pinned launch video on the Bloggo page

**Scene:** Clean setup. Sitting or standing against a plain background. Phone in hand but not always shown. Direct eye contact throughout. This is the definitive first impression.

**Script:**
> "So I want to introduce you to something we've been building.
> It's called Bloggo.
>
> We noticed that most travelers — honestly, all of us —
> come home with hundreds of photos
> and do absolutely nothing with them.
> They sit in your camera roll. The memories fade.
> The story disappears.
>
> Bloggo fixes that.
>
> It scans your existing photos, finds your trips automatically,
> and generates a complete travel blog —
> real place names, AI-written stories for every day and every place.
> In seconds. You don't write a single word.
>
> And if you want to share it?
> Your blog becomes slides — ready for Instagram and TikTok.
>
> [hold up phone]
>
> This is Bloggo.
> Link in bio for early access."

**Delivery:** Warm, personal, confident. Not a pitch — like you're telling a close friend about something you built because it genuinely needed to exist. Pause after "Bloggo fixes that."

**On-screen text:**
- `Bloggo` (large, on name reveal)
- `no writing required`
- `your photos → your blog → your content`

---

## 30s #2 — "The Camera Roll Problem"
**Hook type:** Relatable pain / visual storytelling

**Scene:** Begin by physically holding up your phone and scrolling (camera sees the motion, not the screen). Look up halfway through. Natural, unscripted-feeling.

**Script:**
> "[scrolling phone, holds it up]
> This is my camera roll from a trip I took last year.
>
> Beautiful photos, right?
> Here's what I can't do —
> I can't tell you the name of half the places in these photos.
> I can't describe what happened on day three.
> I couldn't write a paragraph about this trip today.
>
> And I think most of us have this problem.
> We photograph everything — but we capture nothing.
> No story. No context. Just thumbnails.
>
> [puts phone down, leans in]
>
> That's exactly the problem Bloggo solves.
> Your photos become a real blog. Automatically.
> The places are named. The days are organized.
> The story is written.
>
> Comment 'Bloggo' for early access."

**Delivery:** Start reflective and personal. Build to a clean, confident reveal. The "we photograph everything but capture nothing" line is the core — slow down there.

**On-screen text:**
- `"I can't tell you the name of half these places"`
- `we photograph everything`
- `but capture nothing`
- `Bloggo changes that`

---

## 30s #3 — "Blog on the Go"
**Hook type:** Behavior change / feature story

**Scene:** Outdoors if possible, or somewhere with natural light and travel energy. Phone in hand throughout. Can demo the app gesture (opening camera) even without showing the screen clearly.

**Script:**
> "Here's what changed for me the first time I traveled with Bloggo.
>
> Instead of coming home with a thousand unorganized photos
> and the guilt of never writing about any of it —
> I opened Bloggo during the trip.
>
> I take a photo of a restaurant. Bloggo grabs the location.
> I add a quick note. I keep moving.
>
> By the time I'm back at the hotel,
> the story of my day is already half-written.
>
> This is what we call Blogging on the Go —
> building your blog as you live the experience.
> While the details are still real. While the feeling is still there.
>
> [beat]
>
> You'll never look at a photo and think
> 'where even was this' — ever again."

**Delivery:** Storytelling mode. Personal and specific. The phrase "while the details are still real" — slow down here. It's the emotional core of this script.

**On-screen text:**
- `blogging on the go`
- `while the feeling is still fresh`
- `never forget a place again`

---

## 30s #4 — "Before vs. After Bloggo"
**Hook type:** Transformation / contrast

**Scene:** Direct to camera. Clean, simple. Two "modes" — slightly more tired/resigned for the BEFORE, more energized for the AFTER.

**Script:**
> "Let me show you what traveling looks like before and after Bloggo.
>
> Before —
> You take 800 photos. You come home exhausted.
> You tell yourself you'll organize them this weekend.
> Six months later, you're scrolling your camera roll
> and you can't name a single place.
> The trip just... blurs.
>
> After Bloggo —
> You come home to a blog that's already built.
> Every day organized. Every place named.
> A real story written for each location —
> from the photos you already took.
>
> And if you want to post about it?
> Your blog generates slides, ready for Instagram and TikTok.
> Your place names and captions — already included.
>
> [beat]
>
> Your photos always deserved more than a camera roll.
> Bloggo gives them that."

**Delivery:** The "before" section should feel real and slightly heavy — this is genuine pain. The "after" section lifts. End line is slow and sincere — let it land.

**On-screen text:**
- `BEFORE` / `AFTER` as section labels
- `"the trip just... blurs"`
- `your photos always deserved more`

---

## 30s #5 — "Every Photo Had a Reason" *(Emotional / Brand Statement)*
**Hook type:** Philosophical / emotional — high save rate potential

**Scene:** Soft, intimate. Golden hour light if possible. Slower pace than all other videos. This one is the brand soul, not a feature demo.

**Script:**
> "Every photo you've ever taken had a reason.
>
> You took it because something caught your eye.
> Because someone made you laugh.
> Because the light was perfect,
> or the food was unbelievable,
> or you just wanted to hold that moment for a little longer.
>
> And then it went into a camera roll
> with 14,000 other photos.
> And the reason got lost.
>
> Bloggo is built on one idea:
> every photo deserves its story back.
>
> We take your travel photos and give them context —
> a place, a day, a narrative.
> Something real to hold the memory together.
>
> So years from now,
> you don't just see a photo.
> You remember why you took it.
>
> [soft smile]
>
> That's Bloggo."

**Delivery:** This is the slowest, most intentional of all the scripts. No rush. The line "and the reason got lost" — pause before it. "That's Bloggo" at the end should be almost quiet. Let the silence after it sit.

**On-screen text:**
- `every photo had a reason`
- `Bloggo gives it back`
- `every photo. every reason. forever.`
