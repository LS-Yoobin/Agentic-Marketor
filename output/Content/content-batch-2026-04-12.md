## LinkedIn Post #1 — Startup Reality
**Pillar:** Startup Reality
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Voice:** Founder first-person
**Grade:** 34/35 — PUSH ✓

The hardest part of building Bloggo wasn't the code.

It was submitting it and waiting.

You spend months making something. You test it, break it, fix it, polish it.

Then you submit it to the App Store.

And you wait.

Status: "Waiting for Review."

And you realise — there's nothing left to do. It's out of your hands.

For a founder, that's the hardest feeling there is. You want to fix something. Improve something. Do something.

But the work is done. The decision is made.

So I've been doing what I should have done more of all along:

Talking to people who'd actually use it. Sharing the story of why we built it. Sitting with the discomfort of not knowing.

If you're building something right now — this is the part no one talks about.

The waiting is part of the build.

Bloggo is waiting for App Store approval. Following along means more than you know.

## LinkedIn Post #2 — Product Builder Lessons
**Pillar:** Product Builder Lessons
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Voice:** Founder first-person
**Grade:** 31/35 — PUSH ✓

We could have built Bloggo with cloud AI.

Faster to ship. Cheaper to run. Technically simpler.

We didn't.

Bloggo turns your travel photos into blogs — which means it reads your location data, your faces, your private moments.

The question we kept asking ourselves: do we have the right to send all of that to a server?

We decided we didn't.

So we built the whole thing to run on your phone. On-device AI. Offline. Private by design.

It took longer. It's harder to maintain. There are real constraints we live with every day.

But when someone opens Bloggo, their memories stay with them.

That felt like the only acceptable answer.

If you're building a product that handles personal data — this is the conversation you need to have before you write a line of code.
